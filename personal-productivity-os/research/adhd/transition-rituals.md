# Transition Rituals: Bridging the Gap Between Tasks

## The 23-Minute Tax

Research shows that the average person takes 23 minutes and 15 seconds to fully refocus after a task switch. For ADHD brains, this can be significantly longer because of two compounding factors:

1. **Attention residue** — Part of your mind stays stuck on the previous task. The more engaging the previous task was, the harder it is to let go.
2. **Activation energy** — Starting the new task requires its own executive function push, which is already impaired.

The combination means that a simple task switch can cost 30-45 minutes of productive time. In a day with five switches, you can lose half your productive hours to transitions alone.

## Why Transitions Are Uniquely Hard for ADHD

### Letting Go of the Previous Task
If the previous task was interesting, your brain does not want to stop. Hyperfocus does not have an off switch — it has a rip cord. Pulling yourself out of flow feels painful, and the residue lingers.

If the previous task was frustrating, the emotional charge carries into the next task. You bring irritation, defeat, or anxiety with you, contaminating the fresh start.

### The Void Between Tasks
The moment between tasks — when you have finished one thing but not started another — is when ADHD brains are most vulnerable. There is no structure, no momentum, no dopamine. This void is where doomscrolling, snacking, or "just checking" one thing turns into an hour-long detour.

### Loading the New Context
Every task has a mental context: what you are trying to accomplish, where you left off, what tools you need, what constraints exist. Neurotypical brains reload this context relatively quickly. ADHD brains often cannot find the context at all, especially if it has been more than a few hours.

## Transition Rituals That Work

A transition ritual is a brief, consistent routine that creates a boundary between tasks. It signals to your brain: "we are done with that, and we are starting this."

### 1. The Two-Minute Closure
Before leaving a task, spend two minutes:
- Write one sentence about where you stopped
- Write one sentence about what to do next
- Close all related tabs and files

This simple act provides psychological closure and creates a re-entry point for later.

### 2. The Physical Reset
Your body anchors your mental state. Physically moving between tasks helps reset:
- Stand up and stretch
- Walk to a different room (even briefly)
- Wash your face or hands
- Make a drink
- Take three deep breaths

The physical break tells your nervous system that the context has changed.

### 3. The Sensory Shift
Change the sensory environment:
- Switch music or turn it on/off
- Adjust lighting
- Change your workspace (move from desk to couch, or switch rooms)
- Open a window for fresh air

Sensory change signals novelty, which helps the ADHD brain engage with the new context.

### 4. The Intention Statement
Before starting the new task, say or write: "For the next [time period], I am working on [specific task]. The first step is [concrete action]."

This engages the prefrontal cortex, sets a clear target, and provides the activation cue that ADHD brains need.

### 5. The Shutdown Routine (End of Day)
The biggest transition of the day. Without a shutdown routine, work thoughts intrude all evening and sleep suffers.

A simple shutdown:
1. Write tomorrow's top 3 priorities
2. Process your inbox to zero (or close to it)
3. Close all work applications
4. Say (out loud or internally): "The work day is complete."

The verbal or written declaration provides the closure signal that ADHD brains do not generate automatically.

## The Session Boundary Problem

For knowledge workers, the hardest transition is between work sessions on the same project days apart. You worked on the project Monday. Now it is Thursday. What were you doing? What was the next step? What did you decide?

This is not a transition ritual problem — it is a memory problem. And it is where SAME fundamentally changes the game.

- **`create_handoff` IS the closure ritual** — At the end of every session, the handoff captures what was accomplished, what is pending, and any blockers. This is the two-minute closure, automated and persistent.
