# FreshBooks — Invoicing and Accounting

## What It Is

Cloud-based invoicing and accounting software designed for freelancers and small businesses. Handles invoicing, expense tracking, time tracking, and basic accounting. Easier to use than QuickBooks for solo operators.

## Why Freelancers Use It

- Professional invoices in under 2 minutes
- Automatic payment reminders (no more awkward follow-ups)
- Expense tracking with receipt capture
- Built-in time tracking
- Tax-ready reports for your accountant
- Client portal where clients can view and pay invoices

## Setup for Freelancers

1. Sign up at freshbooks.com (30-day free trial)
2. Add your business details and logo
3. Connect your bank account and/or Stripe
4. Set up your default payment terms (Net 15 recommended)
5. Configure automatic payment reminders
6. Create your first invoice template

## Key Settings

- **Payment reminders:** Enable at 3 days before due, on due date, and 7 days after
- **Late fees:** Set to 1.5% monthly (or your contract rate)
- **Online payments:** Connect Stripe or enable FreshBooks Payments
- **Expense categories:** Match to Schedule C categories for tax time
- **Recurring invoices:** Set up for retainer clients

## Tips

- Use the mobile app to photograph receipts immediately
- Set up recurring invoices for retainer clients — set and forget
- Export profit/loss reports quarterly for estimated tax calculations
- Use project tracking to monitor profitability per client
- Save invoice templates for different service types

## Alternatives

- **QuickBooks Self-Employed** — More powerful accounting, steeper learning curve
- **Wave** — Free invoicing and accounting, ad-supported
- **Bonsai** — Freelancer-specific (contracts + invoicing in one tool)
- **Xero** — Popular internationally, strong integrations
