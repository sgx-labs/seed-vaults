# Engineering OKR Examples

## How to Write Good Engineering OKRs

**Objectives** are qualitative and inspirational. **Key Results** are quantitative and measurable. If you can't measure it, it's not a key result.

**Common mistakes:**
- Key results that are just tasks: "Launch feature X" is a task, not a measurement
- Objectives that are metrics: "Increase uptime to 99.9%" is a key result, not an objective
- Too many OKRs: 2-3 objectives with 2-4 key results each. Maximum.

## Team Health & Reliability

**Objective: Our systems are resilient and our team sleeps through the night.**
- KR1: Reduce SEV1/SEV2 incidents from 8/quarter to 3/quarter
- KR2: Achieve 99.95% uptime (from 99.8%)
- KR3: Reduce mean time to recovery from 2 hours to 30 minutes
- KR4: Zero on-call pages between 10pm and 7am

## Developer Productivity

**Objective: Engineers spend their time on high-impact work, not fighting tooling.**
- KR1: Reduce CI pipeline time from 25 minutes to 10 minutes
- KR2: New engineer ships first PR within 3 business days (from 10)
- KR3: Reduce flaky test rate from 12% to under 2%
- KR4: 80% of engineers rate developer experience 4/5 or higher (from 2.8/5)

## Delivery & Speed

**Objective: We ship high-quality features to users faster than ever.**
- KR1: Reduce lead time for changes from 5 days to 1 day
- KR2: Increase deployment frequency from weekly to daily
- KR3: Change failure rate below 10% (from 22%)
- KR4: Ship 3 major features this quarter (defined by product)

## Security & Compliance

**Objective: Security is built into our process, not bolted on afterward.**
- KR1: All production services pass automated security scan with zero critical findings
- KR2: 100% of engineers complete security awareness training
- KR3: Reduce vulnerability remediation time from 30 days to 7 days
- KR4: Achieve SOC2 Type II certification

## Technical Debt

**Objective: Our codebase enables speed instead of slowing us down.**
- KR1: Reduce on-call incident volume related to legacy systems by 50%
- KR2: Migrate 3 highest-cost legacy services to current architecture
- KR3: Eliminate all build dependencies on deprecated libraries
- KR4: Increase test coverage in critical paths from 45% to 80%

## Team Growth

**Objective: We attract and develop the best engineers in our market.**
- KR1: Fill 4 open roles with an average time-to-hire under 45 days
- KR2: Zero regrettable attrition this quarter
- KR3: 100% of engineers have a documented growth plan reviewed in 1-on-1s
- KR4: Publish 4 engineering blog posts (building employer brand)
