# Interview Question Bank

## Behavioral Questions

### Collaboration
- "Tell me about a time you disagreed with a teammate on a technical approach. How did you resolve it?"
- "Describe a situation where you had to get buy-in from someone who initially opposed your idea."
- "Tell me about a project where you depended on another team. How did you manage that dependency?"

### Problem-Solving
- "Tell me about the hardest bug you've ever debugged. Walk me through your process."
- "Describe a time you had to make a technical decision with incomplete information."
- "Tell me about a project where requirements changed significantly mid-way. How did you adapt?"

### Growth & Self-Awareness
- "Tell me about something you were wrong about recently. What did you learn?"
- "What's a technical skill you've intentionally developed in the past year? How?"
- "Describe a piece of feedback you received that changed how you work."

### Leadership (for Senior+ roles)
- "Tell me about a time you identified a problem nobody was addressing and took the initiative to fix it."
- "How have you mentored a junior engineer? What was the outcome?"
- "Describe a technical decision you made that affected multiple teams. How did you build consensus?"

### Stress & Failure
- "Tell me about a production incident you were involved in. What was your role?"
- "Describe a project that failed. What would you do differently?"
- "Tell me about a time you had too much on your plate. How did you prioritize?"

## System Design Questions

### For Mid-Level (IC2-IC3)
- "Design a URL shortener. How would you handle high read volume?"
- "Design a notification system that supports email, push, and SMS."
- "Design a rate limiter for an API. What algorithm would you use?"

### For Senior+ (IC3-IC4)
- "Design a real-time collaborative document editor."
- "Design a distributed task queue with guaranteed delivery."
- "Design a system to process and aggregate millions of events per minute."

### Evaluation Criteria
- Do they ask clarifying questions before diving in?
- Do they consider tradeoffs, not just one solution?
- Do they think about failure modes and edge cases?
- Can they estimate scale and identify bottlenecks?
- Do they communicate their thinking clearly?

## Coding Assessment Options

### Live Coding (45-60 min)
- Practical problem, not algorithm puzzles
- Allow internet access and their preferred IDE
- Focus on code quality, testing approach, and communication
- Good: "Build a simple REST endpoint that does X with these constraints"
- Bad: "Implement a red-black tree from memory"

### Take-Home (2-4 hours max)
- Real-world problem related to your domain
- Clear rubric shared in advance
- Respect their time: set a strict time limit
- Follow up with a review session where they walk through their solution

### Pair Programming (45-60 min)
- Work on a real (simplified) problem from your codebase
- Evaluate how they collaborate, communicate, and handle getting stuck
- Best signal for what it's actually like to work with them

## What to Avoid

- **Brain teasers**: "How many golf balls fit in a school bus?" measures nothing useful.
- **Trivia**: "What's the time complexity of quicksort?" tests memorization, not engineering skill.
- **Hazing**: Making interviews deliberately stressful tests stress tolerance, not job performance.
- **Gotcha questions**: Designed to make candidates fail, not to evaluate their ability.
