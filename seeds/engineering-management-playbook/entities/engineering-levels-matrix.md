# Engineering Levels Matrix

## Individual Contributor Track

| Level | Title | Scope | Autonomy | Impact | Influence |
|-------|-------|-------|----------|--------|-----------|
| IC1 | Junior Engineer | Tasks | Directed: works from detailed specs | Their code | Learning from others |
| IC2 | Engineer | Features | Guided: needs high-level direction | Their features | Contributing to discussions |
| IC3 | Senior Engineer | Systems | Independent: owns workstreams end-to-end | Their team | Mentoring, setting standards |
| IC4 | Staff Engineer | Multi-team | Leading: defines approaches for others | Multiple teams | Driving technical direction |
| IC5 | Senior Staff | Organization | Strategic: shapes org-wide direction | Engineering org | Defining best practices org-wide |
| IC6 | Principal | Company | Visionary: influences company strategy | Company-wide | Industry thought leadership |
| IC7 | Distinguished | Industry | Autonomous: self-directed, industry impact | Industry | Defining state of the art |

## Management Track

| Level | Title | Reports | Scope | Key Responsibilities |
|-------|-------|---------|-------|---------------------|
| M1 | Engineering Manager | 4-8 ICs | One team | People growth, delivery, technical quality |
| M2 | Senior EM | 6-12 or manages EMs | Large team or sub-org | Org design, process, cross-team coordination |
| M3 | Director | Multiple managers | Department | Strategy, headcount planning, exec alignment |
| M4 | VP Engineering | Directors | Major function | Org-wide strategy, budget, executive leadership |
| M5 | SVP/CTO | VPs | Entire eng org | Company strategy, technical vision, board-level |

## IC Level Details

### IC1 — Junior Engineer
- Completes well-defined tasks with clear acceptance criteria
- Asks questions when stuck (and knows when to ask)
- Writes code that passes review without major structural changes
- Learning the codebase, tools, and team norms

### IC2 — Engineer
- Delivers features independently from design to deployment
- Writes tests, handles edge cases, considers error scenarios
- Participates in code reviews and provides useful feedback
- Estimates work with reasonable accuracy

### IC3 — Senior Engineer
- Owns large features or system components end-to-end
- Makes sound technical decisions with minimal guidance
- Mentors junior engineers through pairing and review
- Identifies and addresses technical debt proactively
- Communicates technical decisions clearly to non-technical stakeholders

### IC4 — Staff Engineer
- Drives technical direction across their team and adjacent teams
- Solves ambiguous, cross-cutting problems that don't have obvious owners
- Writes RFCs and ADRs that shape architectural decisions
- Multiplies the team's effectiveness (their absence would be felt org-wide)
- Balances ideal solutions with pragmatic constraints

### IC5+ — Senior Staff and Above
- Influences technical strategy at the organizational level
- Identifies and drives multi-quarter technical initiatives
- Represents the company externally (talks, open source, publications)
- Mentors Staff engineers and shapes engineering culture
- Works across organizational boundaries as a technical leader

## Common Calibration Mistakes

- **Confusing tenure with level**: 5 years at IC2 doesn't make someone IC3. Impact determines level, not time served.
- **Over-indexing on technical skill**: IC4+ requires influence and communication, not just coding ability.
- **Different standards across teams**: Ensure cross-team calibration so "Senior" means the same thing everywhere.
- **Title inflation**: Giving everyone "Senior" titles to retain them creates calibration chaos.
