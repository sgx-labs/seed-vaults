# Team Health Indicators

## Overview

Most managers notice team health problems when someone quits, a project fails, or morale visibly collapses. By then it's too late. The skill is reading leading indicators -- signals that predict problems weeks or months before they surface. This note is your early warning system.

## Leading Indicators (Things That Predict Problems)

### Communication Patterns
- **Silence in retros**: When people stop raising issues, they haven't stopped having them. They've stopped believing anything will change.
- **Backchannel complaints**: If issues reach you through side conversations instead of direct channels, your team doesn't feel safe raising them publicly.
- **Declining Slack engagement**: Less chatter, fewer emoji reactions, more terse responses. People are disengaging.
- **Meetings where one person talks**: If the same 2-3 people dominate every discussion, the rest have checked out.

### Work Patterns
- **Rising WIP (work in progress)**: When individuals have 4+ items in flight, nothing's getting finished. This is a coordination problem, not a productivity problem.
- **Increasing cycle time**: PRs sitting longer, tickets taking more sprints. The work isn't getting harder -- the team is getting stuck.
- **Heroics becoming normal**: If shipping requires someone working weekends, your process is broken. One-time heroics are fine. Repeated heroics are a system failure.
- **Declining code review quality**: Rubber-stamp approvals, no substantive comments. People are too busy or don't care enough.

### People Patterns
- **1-on-1 cancellations**: When your reports cancel 1-on-1s, they're either overwhelmed or disengaged. Both are problems.
- **PTO avoidance**: People not taking vacation means they're afraid to step away. People taking lots of unexpected sick days means they're burning out.
- **Reduced learning investment**: No one's reading, attending talks, or exploring new things. They're in survival mode.
- **Departure questions**: "How does the equity vesting work again?" or "What's our reference policy?" are exit signals.

## Lagging Indicators (Too Late to Prevent)

These confirm problems that already exist:
- Resignations
- Failed projects
- Customer complaints about quality
- Visible conflicts between team members
- Missed deadlines becoming the norm

## The Team Health Check

Run this quarterly. Takes 15 minutes per person. Anonymous survey or direct conversation.

**Five questions that actually matter:**
1. "Do you feel like you're learning and growing?" (Growth)
2. "Do you feel safe raising problems without consequences?" (Safety)
3. "Do you understand why we're building what we're building?" (Purpose)
4. "Is your workload sustainable?" (Load)
5. "Would you recommend this team to a friend?" (Overall)

Score 1-5. Track trends over time. The absolute number matters less than the direction.

## When NOT to Use This Framework

- **Don't turn it into surveillance**: Monitoring Slack activity or PR metrics to "catch" problems creates the exact environment you're trying to prevent.
- **Don't over-index on one signal**: A quiet week in Slack might mean everyone's heads-down on a deadline, not that morale collapsed.
- **Don't skip the conversation**: Metrics are a prompt for conversation, not a replacement for it.

## In Reality

You'll notice some of these signals and rationalize them away. "They're just busy." "It's a hard quarter." "That's just how they are." Challenge your own rationalizations. If something feels off, it probably is.

The single best health check: trust your gut, then verify with data and conversation.
