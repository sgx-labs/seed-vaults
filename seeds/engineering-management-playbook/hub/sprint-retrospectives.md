# Sprint Retrospective Formats

## Overview

Retrospectives are how teams learn. A good retro surfaces problems, celebrates wins, and produces concrete actions. A bad retro is a ritual where everyone says "things are fine" for 30 minutes. If your retros feel stale, the format is probably the problem. Here are five proven formats -- rotate them to keep the conversation fresh.

## Format 1: Start / Stop / Continue

**Best for**: Teams new to retros, or when you need a simple reset.
**Time**: 30 minutes.

1. **Silent brainstorm (5 min)**: Everyone writes sticky notes for three columns: Start doing, Stop doing, Continue doing
2. **Group and discuss (15 min)**: Cluster similar items, discuss the top ones
3. **Vote and commit (10 min)**: Dot-vote on the top 3 actions. Assign owners.

**When NOT to use**: When the team is comfortable with retros and this feels too basic. It can produce generic observations if the team isn't pushed to be specific.

## Format 2: 4Ls — Liked, Learned, Lacked, Longed For

**Best for**: After a major milestone or release. More reflective than Start/Stop/Continue.
**Time**: 45 minutes.

1. **Liked**: What went well? What should we celebrate?
2. **Learned**: What did we discover? New skills, insights, or surprises?
3. **Lacked**: What was missing? Tools, context, support, communication?
4. **Longed For**: What do we wish we had? What would make next sprint better?

**When NOT to use**: In a crisis. This format is reflective and doesn't generate urgency.

## Format 3: Sailboat

**Best for**: Visual thinkers, teams that are stuck in a rut, or when you want to talk about both forces and blockers.
**Time**: 45 minutes.

Draw a sailboat on a whiteboard:
- **Wind (sails)**: What's pushing us forward?
- **Anchors**: What's holding us back?
- **Rocks (underwater)**: What risks are ahead that we can't see yet?
- **Island (destination)**: Where are we trying to get?

This format naturally produces strategic conversation, not just tactical gripes.

**When NOT to use**: When you need quick, tactical action items. This format goes deep but can be slow to converge.

## Format 4: Timeline

**Best for**: After a long sprint, a complex project, or when you want to understand what happened and when.
**Time**: 60 minutes.

1. Draw a timeline of the sprint on the wall
2. Everyone adds sticky notes at specific points: events, feelings, blockers, wins
3. Walk through chronologically
4. Identify patterns: "We always hit a wall mid-sprint because..."

**When NOT to use**: For routine 2-week sprints. The overhead of reconstructing a timeline isn't worth it for short, uneventful cycles.

## Format 5: Mad / Sad / Glad

**Best for**: When you suspect emotional undercurrents. This format gives explicit permission to talk about feelings.
**Time**: 30 minutes.

1. **Mad**: What frustrated you? What made you angry?
2. **Sad**: What disappointed you? What do you wish had gone differently?
3. **Glad**: What made you happy? What are you proud of?

This format surfaces things that pure process retros miss. When someone says "I'm sad that nobody reviewed my PR for three days," that's a signal Start/Stop/Continue might not catch.

**When NOT to use**: If the team doesn't have psychological safety yet. Asking for vulnerability without trust backfires.

## Making Retros Actually Work

Regardless of format:

1. **Rotate the facilitator**: Not always the manager. Let ICs run them.
2. **Silent writing first**: Always start with individual brainstorming. Otherwise the loudest person dominates.
3. **Limit to 3 action items**: More than 3 and nothing gets done.
4. **Review last retro's actions first**: Start every retro by checking if previous actions were completed. If they weren't, that's this retro's first discussion topic.
5. **Manager speaks last**: Your opinion has outsized influence. Let others go first.

## In Reality

Your team will eventually say "retros are a waste of time." This usually means one of two things: the actions never get followed up on (so why bother raising issues?), or the format has gone stale. Fix the follow-through first. Change the format second.
