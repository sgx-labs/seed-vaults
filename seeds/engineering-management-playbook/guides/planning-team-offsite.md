# Planning a Team Offsite

## Why Offsites Matter

For remote and hybrid teams, the offsite is your highest-leverage investment in team cohesion. In-person time builds trust that sustains months of remote work. For co-located teams, offsites break routine and create space for strategic thinking.

## Planning Timeline

### 6-8 Weeks Out
- **Set the goal**: Social bonding? Strategic planning? Both? Be clear.
- **Pick dates**: Avoid holidays, on-call conflicts, major releases
- **Book venue**: Prefer a space with both meeting rooms and casual areas
- **Set budget**: Travel, accommodation, food, activities. Get approval early.
- **Announce**: Give people time to arrange personal logistics

### 3-4 Weeks Out
- **Draft agenda**: Share with the team for input
- **Assign session owners**: Not just the manager presenting for 3 days
- **Book travel**: For remote team members
- **Dietary restrictions and accessibility needs**: Ask explicitly

### 1 Week Out
- **Final agenda shared**: With links to any pre-read material
- **Logistics doc**: Venue address, wifi info, restaurant reservations, emergency contacts
- **Reminder**: "Looking forward to seeing everyone. Here's what to expect."

## Agenda Design

### The 3:2:1 Ratio
For a 2-day offsite: 3 working sessions, 2 social activities, 1 unstructured block.

**Working sessions** (90 min each):
- Strategic discussion: "Where should we focus next quarter?"
- Retrospective: Deeper than a sprint retro -- team norms, collaboration, culture
- Hackathon or technical exploration: Build something fun, learn something new

**Social activities** (2-3 hours each):
- Shared meal (required -- eating together builds connection faster than any exercise)
- Group activity: cooking class, hiking, escape room, game night. Not mandatory "fun."

**Unstructured time** (2+ hours):
- Free time to rest, explore, or have informal conversations
- This is where the best ideas and deepest connections happen. Don't overschedule.

### What NOT to Do

- **Don't fill every minute**: Overscheduled offsites are exhausting, not energizing
- **Don't do normal sprint work**: If people are at their laptops doing tickets, why are you off-site?
- **Don't force "team building exercises"**: Trust falls and "two truths and a lie" feel patronizing. Let connection happen naturally through shared meals and activities.
- **Don't make it all work**: An offsite that's 100% presentations isn't an offsite -- it's a conference you were forced to attend.
- **Don't forget introverts**: Include solo time. Not everyone recharges through group activities.

## Budget Tips

- **Off-peak timing**: Avoid conference season and holidays
- **Airbnb/house rental**: Often cheaper and more flexible than hotels for small teams
- **Local team members host**: If someone's in the offsite city, they can recommend venues and restaurants
- **Justify the cost**: "This offsite costs $15K. Replacing one engineer who quits due to low team cohesion costs $50K+ in recruiting and ramp-up."

## After the Offsite

- **Share notes and action items** within 48 hours
- **Follow up on commitments** in your next team meeting
- **Gather feedback**: "What worked? What would you change?" (anonymous is better)
- **Plan the next one**: Quarterly for remote teams, semi-annually for co-located
