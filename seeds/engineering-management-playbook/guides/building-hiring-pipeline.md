# Building a Hiring Pipeline

## The Full Pipeline

### Stage 1: Define the Role (Before You Start)

- **Write a clear job description** focused on what they'll DO, not a wish list of technologies
- **Define the level** using your levels matrix (see `entities/engineering-levels-matrix.md`)
- **Set the compensation range** before you start interviewing (see `research/compensation-and-leveling.md`)
- **Identify must-haves vs nice-to-haves**: No more than 3 must-haves. Everything else is flexible.
- **Create the rubric** (see `hub/hiring-rubric.md`)

### Stage 2: Source Candidates

**Channels (in order of typical quality):**
1. Employee referrals (but watch for homogeneity -- see `research/diversity-inclusion-hiring.md`)
2. Inbound from your engineering brand (blog, open source, talks)
3. Recruiter sourcing (LinkedIn, specialized platforms)
4. Job boards (targeted: Hacker News Who's Hiring, specialized communities)
5. Agency recruiters (expensive but fast for urgent roles)

**Pipeline math**: You typically need 50-100 top-of-funnel candidates to make 1 hire. Plan accordingly.

### Stage 3: Screen (30 min)

Recruiter or hiring manager phone screen:
- Motivation: Why this role? Why now?
- Experience fit: Does their background match the level?
- Logistics: Location, compensation expectations, timeline, visa needs
- Red flags: Misaligned expectations, concerning pattern of short tenures (ask, don't assume)

**Pass rate target**: 30-40% move forward. If it's higher, your sourcing is good. If it's lower, your sourcing is misaligned.

### Stage 4: Technical Assessment (1-2 hours)

Choose one:
- **Live coding session** (45-60 min): Practical problem, their preferred environment
- **Take-home project** (2-4 hours): Real-world problem, followed by review session
- **System design** (45-60 min): For senior+ roles

See `entities/interview-question-bank.md` for question templates.

### Stage 5: Team Interviews (2-3 sessions, 45 min each)

Each interviewer covers a different rubric dimension:
- Interviewer 1: Collaboration and communication
- Interviewer 2: Technical depth and system thinking
- Interviewer 3: Growth trajectory and values alignment

### Stage 6: Debrief and Decision (30 min)

- Written scores submitted BEFORE the meeting
- Each interviewer shares their top evidence
- Discuss disagreements
- Hiring manager makes the final call
- If the group is split, default to no-hire

### Stage 7: Offer and Close

- **Move fast**: The best candidates have multiple offers. Get an offer out within 48 hours of the decision.
- **Personal touch**: The hiring manager calls with the offer, not just an email.
- **Address concerns**: Ask "What would make this a clear yes for you?"
- **Give them time**: Don't pressure. 1 week to decide is reasonable.

## Metrics to Track

| Metric | Target | Why It Matters |
|--------|--------|---------------|
| Time to hire | 30-45 days | Longer = losing candidates to faster companies |
| Offer acceptance rate | > 80% | Lower = comp, culture, or experience problem |
| Pipeline diversity | Track by stage | Where do underrepresented candidates drop off? |
| Interviewer calibration | Track pass rates | Identify overly harsh or lenient interviewers |
| New hire 6-month retention | > 90% | Measures hiring quality, not just speed |
