# Running Your First 1-on-1

## Before the Meeting

### Prepare (15 minutes)

1. **Create a shared doc** with both your names. Share edit access. This becomes your ongoing 1-on-1 document.

2. **Add a light agenda** to the doc:
   ```
   First 1-on-1 — [Date]
   - Getting to know each other
   - How they like to work
   - What they need from me
   - Questions for me
   ```

3. **Review what you know**: Their role, recent projects, tenure, anything notable from their hiring process or previous manager's notes.

4. **Check your nerves**: If this is your first time managing, you're probably more nervous than they are. That's normal. You don't need to have all the answers.

### Set the Tone

Send a brief message beforehand: "Looking forward to our first 1-on-1. I've set up a shared doc [link]. This is your meeting -- I want to hear what matters to you. No prep needed on your end."

## During the Meeting (30 minutes)

### Opening (10 min) — Get to Know Each Other

Don't jump into work topics. Start human:

- "Tell me about your background. How did you end up here?"
- "What do you enjoy most about your work?"
- "What's been your experience with 1-on-1s in the past?"

Share something about yourself too. This isn't an interview -- it's a relationship.

### Working Style (10 min) — Set Expectations

Ask:
- "How do you like to receive feedback? Real-time, or do you prefer to process first?"
- "What does a good week look like for you? What does a bad week look like?"
- "How do you prefer to communicate? Slack, email, tap on the shoulder?"
- "Is there anything you need from me that you haven't been getting?"

Share your own answers to these questions. Transparency goes both ways.

### Their Priorities (10 min) — Listen

- "What are you working on right now that you're excited about?"
- "What's frustrating you or slowing you down?"
- "Is there anything you've wanted to tell a manager but haven't had the chance?"
- "What would make these 1-on-1s valuable for you?"

Take notes in the shared doc. This demonstrates that you're listening and will follow through.

## After the Meeting

- **Within 24 hours**: Follow up on anything you committed to. Even small things. First impressions of reliability matter.
- **Set the cadence**: Schedule a recurring 30-minute weekly slot. Same time, same day. Protect it.

## What NOT to Do in Your First 1-on-1

- Don't turn it into a status update
- Don't talk about yourself the whole time
- Don't make promises you're not sure you can keep
- Don't pretend to know things you don't
- Don't skip it or reschedule because "something came up"

## If You're Taking Over from Another Manager

The dynamic is different. They have expectations (good or bad) from their previous manager.

Additional questions:
- "What worked well with your previous manager?"
- "What would you change about how you were managed?"
- "Is there any context I should have about ongoing projects or situations?"

Listen for what's unsaid. If they were managed poorly, trust will take time.
