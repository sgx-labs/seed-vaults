# Writing a Promotion Packet

## What a Promotion Packet Is

A promotion packet is a structured argument that someone is consistently performing at the next level. It's evidence-based, not opinion-based. The packet convinces a calibration committee (or your manager's manager) that this person has earned the promotion -- it's not a request, it's documentation of what's already true.

## Step 1: Verify Readiness

Before writing, ask honestly:

- Is this person consistently performing at the next level, not just occasionally?
- Can I point to 3+ significant examples from the past 6-12 months?
- Would a peer at the target level recognize this person as an equal?
- Have I received positive signal from others (peers, cross-team stakeholders)?

If any answer is "no," the person isn't ready. That's okay -- help them get there. Don't submit a weak packet, as it burns credibility and disappoints the candidate.

## Step 2: Gather Evidence

For each dimension at the target level (see `entities/engineering-levels-matrix.md`), collect:

- **Specific projects** with measurable outcomes
- **Peer feedback** (formal and informal)
- **Technical artifacts** (RFCs, designs, code reviews, documentation)
- **Leadership evidence** (mentoring, leading initiatives, influencing decisions)
- **Scope increase** (taking on bigger problems, broader impact)

## Step 3: Write the Packet

### Structure

```
# Promotion Packet: [Name], [Current Level] -> [Target Level]

**Submitted by**: [Your name]
**Date**: [Date]
**Review period**: [Start date] to [End date]

## Summary
[2-3 sentences: who this person is, what they've accomplished, and why
they're ready for the next level.]

## Evidence by Dimension

### Scope
[Example 1: What they did, the scope of impact, measurable outcome]
[Example 2: ...]

### Technical Skill
[Example 1: Complex problem solved, approach taken, quality of solution]
[Example 2: ...]

### Leadership & Influence
[Example 1: How they influenced beyond their immediate work]
[Example 2: ...]

### Communication
[Example 1: How they communicated effectively in a high-stakes situation]

## Peer Feedback
[3-4 quotes from colleagues, including at least one from outside the team]

## Growth Trajectory
[How they've grown during the review period. What's different now vs 12
months ago?]

## Areas for Continued Growth
[Honest assessment of what they'll need to develop at the new level. This
adds credibility -- nobody is perfect.]
```

## Step 4: Review and Submit

- **Self-review with the candidate**: Show them the packet (or a summary). Ask: "Does this accurately represent your work? What am I missing?"
- **Peer review with a fellow manager**: Ask someone who knows the target level to read it. "Does this sound like a [target level] to you?"
- **Submit with conviction**: If you're not confident in the packet, don't submit it. Strengthen it or wait.

## Common Mistakes

- **Listing tasks, not impact**: "Worked on the payment migration" vs "Led the payment migration that reduced checkout failures by 34%"
- **Recency bias**: Using only the last month's work. Go back 6-12 months.
- **Conflating activity with level**: "They worked really hard" is not evidence of the next level.
- **No outside perspective**: Internal-only evidence is weaker. Cross-team impact is stronger signal.
- **Ignoring growth areas**: A packet with zero weaknesses isn't honest -- it's advocacy without credibility.
