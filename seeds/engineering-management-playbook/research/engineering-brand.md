# Building an Engineering Brand

## Why Bother

An engineering brand -- tech blog, open source contributions, conference talks, community presence -- makes hiring easier, retention better, and your team prouder. Engineers want to work at places they've heard good things about. Your brand is the first impression.

## The Three Pillars

### 1. Tech Blog

**What works:**
- Real engineering stories, not marketing fluff. "How we migrated 10M rows without downtime" not "Our amazing tech stack."
- Honest postmortems. Nothing builds credibility faster than publicly owning a failure and explaining what you learned.
- Written by engineers, not content marketers. Authenticity matters.
- Consistent cadence: 1-2 posts per month is plenty. Quality over quantity.

**Getting engineers to write:**
- Pair a writer with an editor (manager or technical writer) who handles polish
- Allocate explicit time: "2 hours this sprint to draft your blog post" not "do it on your own time"
- Make it optional but celebrated. Recognize authors in team meetings and company channels.
- Topic ideas from retros, postmortems, and design discussions -- the content already exists

**Common mistakes:**
- Forcing everyone to write (not everyone wants to, and forced writing is bad writing)
- Editorial approval that strips out the interesting parts
- Only writing about successes (failure stories are more valuable and more readable)

### 2. Open Source

**Levels of engagement:**

1. **Use and contribute back**: Fix bugs in libraries you depend on. Low effort, high goodwill.
2. **Extract and publish**: Internal tools that aren't competitive advantages. Open source the developer tools, linters, or libraries that others could use.
3. **Build in the open**: New projects developed as open source from day one.

**Making it sustainable:**
- Dedicated maintenance time (at least a few hours per week for active projects)
- Clear contribution guidelines and responsive maintainers
- Don't open source and abandon -- that damages your brand more than not open sourcing

### 3. Conference Talks and Community

**Getting started:**
- Start with local meetups (lower bar than major conferences)
- Lightning talks (5-10 min) for people who've never spoken
- Conference CFP workshops: help engineers write good proposals
- Sponsor events your team cares about

**Support structure:**
- Rehearsal sessions where colleagues give feedback
- Travel budget and conference days (not vacation days)
- Celebrate speakers the way you celebrate shippers

## When NOT to Invest in Engineering Brand

- **When the product is struggling**: Fix your product first. An engineering blog from a company with a failing product is tone-deaf.
- **When the team is burned out**: Brand building is extra work. Don't add to an overloaded team.
- **As a substitute for good management**: A great blog doesn't compensate for bad comp, poor culture, or terrible on-call.
- **Inauthentically**: If you have to hide working conditions to make the brand attractive, the brand is a lie.

## In Reality

Engineering brand compounds slowly. The blog post you write today gets referenced by a candidate in their interview six months from now. The open source project you ship today attracts a contributor who becomes your next hire a year from now. It's a long game, but it's one of the highest-ROI investments a mature engineering team can make.
