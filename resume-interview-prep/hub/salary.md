# Salary Negotiation

## Overview

Negotiation is expected and respected. Failing to negotiate leaves money on the table — often $5,000-$15,000+ annually. This hub covers researching market rates for your role, handling the "what are your salary expectations?" question, structuring a counter-offer, negotiating beyond base salary (bonus, equity, PTO, remote, signing bonus), evaluating total compensation packages, and knowing when to accept vs walk away.

## Key Principles

1. **Never name a number first.** Let the company make the first offer whenever possible.
2. **Research the range.** Use Levels.fyi, Glassdoor, Payscale, and network contacts.
3. **Negotiate total comp, not just salary.** Signing bonus, equity, PTO, remote days, title, start date.
4. **Get it in writing.** Verbal offers mean nothing. Wait for the written offer letter.
5. **Be enthusiastic, then negotiate.** Express genuine excitement first, then discuss numbers.

## Quick Reference — Negotiation Framework

```
1. Receive offer → express enthusiasm → ask for time to review
2. Research comparable roles → determine your range
3. Counter with specific number + justification
4. If they can't move on salary → negotiate other items
5. Get final offer in writing → accept formally
```
