# Cover Letters

## Overview

A cover letter is your chance to tell the story your resume cannot. It connects the dots between your experience and the specific role, demonstrates genuine interest in the company, and shows personality that a bullet-point list cannot convey. This hub covers when a cover letter actually matters, the three-paragraph structure that works, customizing for each company without starting from scratch, opening lines that grab attention, and closing with a clear call to action.

## Key Principles

1. **Never send a generic cover letter.** If you cannot customize it, skip it entirely.
2. **Answer "why this company" specifically.** Show you researched them. Reference a product, value, or recent news.
3. **Tell a story your resume cannot.** Connect experiences, explain transitions, show motivation.
4. **Keep it under one page.** Three to four paragraphs. Respect the reader's time.

## Quick Reference — Three-Paragraph Structure

```
Paragraph 1: Hook + why this specific company/role
Paragraph 2: Your strongest relevant experience + impact (bridge to role)
Paragraph 3: What you'll bring + call to action
```
