# Resume Writing & Tailoring

## Overview

Your resume is a marketing document, not a biography. It exists to get you past the ATS filter and into a human's hands, then to convince that human you are worth a 30-minute conversation. This hub covers tailoring your resume to specific job postings, writing quantified accomplishment-driven bullet points, ATS-friendly formatting, strategic keyword placement, choosing the right resume format (chronological, functional, combination), handling employment gaps and career transitions, and the one-page vs two-page decision. Every bullet point should answer: "So what? What was the impact?"

## Key Principles

1. **Tailor for every application.** Mirror the language and priorities of the job posting.
2. **Lead with impact, not duties.** "Managed a team" is a duty. "Led 8-person team that shipped $2M product 3 weeks early" is impact.
3. **Quantify everything.** Revenue, percentages, team size, time saved, cost reduced, users served.
4. **Front-load the good stuff.** Recruiters spend 6-7 seconds on first scan. Put your strongest content first.
5. **ATS before aesthetics.** A beautiful resume that gets filtered out is worthless. Clean formatting, standard headers.

## Quick Reference — Bullet Point Formula

```
[Strong Action Verb] + [What You Did] + [For Whom/What] + [Measurable Result]

Example:
"Spearheaded migration of 3 legacy systems to cloud infrastructure, reducing operational costs by 40% and improving uptime from 99.2% to 99.9%"
```
