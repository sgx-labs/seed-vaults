# Company Research

## Overview

Knowing the company is the difference between a generic interview and one where you demonstrate genuine fit. This hub covers how to research a target company's mission, values, and culture, understanding the team structure and who you would report to, analyzing recent news, earnings, and strategic direction, reading Glassdoor and Blind reviews critically, identifying the company's pain points that you can solve, and building a company research note for each target employer.

## Key Principles

1. **Go beyond the About page.** Read earnings calls, press releases, LinkedIn posts from team members.
2. **Understand their problems.** The job posting tells you what they need solved. Research why.
3. **Know the competition.** Understanding industry context shows strategic thinking.
4. **Talk to people who work there.** One conversation beats ten hours of web research.

## Quick Reference — Company Research Checklist

```
□ Mission and values statement
□ Recent news and press releases (last 6 months)
□ Key products/services and competitive position
□ Team structure — who you'd report to and work with
□ Glassdoor/Blind reviews (look for patterns, not outliers)
□ LinkedIn profiles of interviewers and team members
□ Recent job postings (what else are they hiring for? signals growth areas)
□ Financial health (public: earnings; private: funding rounds)
```
