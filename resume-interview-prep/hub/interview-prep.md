# Interview Preparation

## Overview

Interviews are won in the preparation, not in the moment. This hub covers the STAR method for behavioral interviews, preparing your "tell me about yourself" pitch, researching the interviewer and team, common behavioral question categories (leadership, conflict, failure, teamwork, initiative), technical and case interview preparation, questions to ask the interviewer that demonstrate genuine interest, handling curveball and stress questions, video interview best practices, and post-interview follow-up strategy. The goal is not to memorize scripts but to have a library of stories ready to deploy.

## Key Principles

1. **Prepare stories, not scripts.** Have 8-10 STAR stories that cover the major behavioral categories.
2. **Research the interviewer.** LinkedIn, company blog, published talks — find common ground.
3. **Ask thoughtful questions.** Your questions reveal more about you than your answers do.
4. **Follow up within 24 hours.** A specific, personalized thank-you email is not optional.
5. **Practice out loud.** Thinking through an answer and saying it aloud are completely different skills.

## Quick Reference — STAR Method

```
S — Situation: Set the scene (brief context)
T — Task: What was your specific responsibility?
A — Action: What did YOU do? (not the team — you)
R — Result: What happened? Quantify the outcome.

Keep it to 90 seconds. End on the result.
```
