# ATS — Applicant Tracking Systems

## What It Is
Software that companies use to collect, filter, and rank job applications. Common systems include Workday, Greenhouse, Lever, iCIMS, and Taleo. 75%+ of large companies use an ATS.

## How ATS Works
1. You submit your resume through the application portal
2. ATS parses your resume into structured data fields (name, contact, experience, education, skills)
3. System scores your resume against the job description keywords
4. Resumes above the threshold are forwarded to the hiring manager
5. Resumes below the threshold may never be seen by a human

## Key Facts
- **Keyword matching is literal** — "project management" and "managing projects" may score differently
- **Formatting matters** — tables, columns, and images break parsing
- **File format matters** — .docx is safest for ATS; some accept .pdf
- **Standard section headers** — use "Experience," "Education," "Skills" — not creative alternatives
- **Headers/footers are often skipped** — put contact info in the body

## Common ATS by Company Size
- **Enterprise:** Workday, Taleo (Oracle), SuccessFactors (SAP)
- **Mid-market:** Greenhouse, Lever, iCIMS, BambooHR
- **Startups:** Lever, Greenhouse, Ashby
