# How to Research a Target Company

## The Core Idea

Deep company research transforms you from a generic candidate into someone who clearly "gets it." Interviewers notice when a candidate understands their challenges, values, and strategic direction. Spend 1-2 hours researching before any interview.

## Research Layers

### Layer 1: The Basics (15 minutes)
- Company website: About, Mission, Values, Leadership
- Wikipedia for history and key facts
- Recent news (last 6 months)
- Size, revenue, locations, industry position

### Layer 2: The Culture (30 minutes)
- Glassdoor reviews — look for patterns across 20+ reviews, not individual complaints
- Blind (for tech companies) — unfiltered employee sentiment
- LinkedIn: browse employee profiles, see where they came from and where they go
- Company blog and social media — what do they celebrate and promote?
- Their careers page — how do they describe themselves to candidates?

### Layer 3: The Role (30 minutes)
- Decode the job posting — required vs preferred, keywords, reporting structure
- Find the hiring manager on LinkedIn — their background tells you what they value
- Look for similar roles the company has posted — same role reposted signals urgency or turnover
- Check if you know anyone who works there or has worked there

### Layer 4: Strategic Context (30 minutes)
- For public companies: latest earnings call, 10-K highlights, analyst reports
- For private companies: Crunchbase funding history, recent press
- Competitors and market position
- Industry trends affecting them

## Creating a Company Research Note

After researching, create a note in `entities/[company-name].md` with:
- Company overview and key facts
- Culture signals (positive and concerning)
- Role analysis and likely interview focus areas
- People to know (hiring manager, team leads)
- Questions you want to ask
- Talking points that connect your experience to their needs
