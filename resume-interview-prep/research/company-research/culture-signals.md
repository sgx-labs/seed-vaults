# Finding Company Culture Signals

## The Core Idea

Every company says they have "great culture." What matters is evidence. Culture signals come from how a company operates, not what it claims in a values statement. Learning to read these signals helps you evaluate fit and prepare better interview answers.

## Positive Signals

- **Low turnover in the specific team** — people stay because they like it
- **Internal promotions visible on LinkedIn** — people grow here
- **Specific, not generic, values** — "We ship weekly" beats "We value innovation"
- **Employees posting about work positively** — without being forced to
- **Investment in learning** — conference budgets, internal training, education benefits
- **Transparent communication** — public roadmaps, open all-hands, published compensation bands

## Warning Signs

- **Constant hiring for the same role** — turnover or poor management
- **"We're a family"** — often means boundary issues and guilt-driven overwork
- **Emphasis on "passion"** — may signal expectations of unpaid overtime
- **No mention of work-life balance** — they may not have any
- **Glassdoor reviews mentioning "politics"** — dysfunction in leadership
- **Job postings full of buzzwords** — they may not know what they actually need

## How to Ask About Culture in Interviews

Do not ask "What is the culture like?" — you will get a rehearsed answer.

Instead, ask:
- "Can you walk me through a typical week on the team?"
- "How does the team handle disagreements about approach?"
- "What has surprised you most about working here?"
- "How is feedback given — both positive and constructive?"
- "What does work-life balance actually look like for this team?"
