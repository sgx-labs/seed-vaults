# Reading Job Postings Like a Hiring Manager

## The Core Idea

Job postings are wish lists, not checklists. Hiring managers write their ideal candidate, then hire the best available person. If you match 60-70% of the requirements, apply. Understanding how to decode a posting gives you a tailoring advantage.

## How to Read a Job Posting

### The First Paragraph
This is the "hook" — it reveals what the team actually needs. Words like "fast-paced," "ambiguous," or "build from scratch" signal a startup-y culture even in big companies.

### Requirements Section
- **Must-haves** are listed first and repeated multiple times
- **Nice-to-haves** are usually labeled as "preferred" or "bonus"
- **Years of experience** is flexible — they want the skills, not a specific number
- If a tool or skill appears more than once, it is critical

### Responsibilities Section
- The first 3 responsibilities are the actual job
- The rest are aspirational padding
- Look for verbs: "manage" vs "support" vs "assist" signals seniority level

### Hidden Signals
- "Wear many hats" = under-resourced team, broad scope
- "Self-starter" = minimal onboarding and management
- "Data-driven" = they want metrics and analysis
- "Cross-functional" = you will need to work with other teams
- "Excellent communication skills" = the role involves stakeholder management
- Listing many tools = they do not know what they need, or the stack is in flux

## The 60% Rule

If you meet 60% or more of the requirements, apply. If you wait for 100% matches, you will apply to very few jobs. The posting describes the ideal — companies regularly hire people who are strong in some areas and can grow in others.
