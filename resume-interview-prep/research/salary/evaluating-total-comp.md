# Evaluating Total Compensation

## The Core Idea

Base salary is one piece of the puzzle. Two offers with the same base can differ by $20,000+ in total value when you factor in benefits, bonuses, equity, and perks. Always evaluate the full package.

## Components of Total Compensation

### Cash Compensation
- **Base salary** — your guaranteed annual pay
- **Annual bonus** — target percentage and how realistic it is to hit
- **Signing bonus** — one-time, often used to bridge a gap in base salary negotiation

### Equity (If Applicable)
- **RSUs** — granted shares that vest over time (typically 4-year schedule)
- **Stock options** — right to buy at a set price (more common at startups)
- **Evaluate at current value** — do not count on stock appreciation

### Benefits
- **Health insurance** — premiums, deductible, coverage quality (can vary by $3,000-$8,000/year)
- **401(k) match** — free money. A 6% match on $100K salary = $6,000/year
- **PTO** — count the days and the culture around actually using them
- **Parental leave** — if relevant now or in the future

### Perks and Flexibility
- **Remote work** — saves commute time and money ($3,000-$8,000/year in many cities)
- **Professional development** — conference budget, tuition reimbursement
- **Wellness stipend** — gym, mental health, equipment

## How to Compare Offers

Create a simple comparison sheet:
```
                    Offer A         Offer B
Base salary         $95,000         $90,000
Bonus (target)      10% ($9,500)    15% ($13,500)
Signing bonus       $0              $5,000
401(k) match        3% ($2,850)     6% ($5,400)
Health premium      $200/mo         $100/mo
PTO                 15 days         20 days
Remote              Hybrid (3/2)    Full remote
───────────────────────────────────────────
Est. Total Year 1   ~$105,950       ~$112,700
```
