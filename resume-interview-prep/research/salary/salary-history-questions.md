# Handling Salary History Questions

## The Core Idea

Salary history questions anchor the negotiation to your past pay, not your market value. Many states have banned them. Regardless of legality, you are better off redirecting the conversation to the role's value and your expectations.

## Deflection Scripts

### "What is your current salary?"
- "I'd prefer to focus on the value I can bring to this role. What is the budgeted range for this position?"
- "My current compensation reflects a different role and market. I'm happy to discuss expectations once I understand the full scope of this position."
- "I'm not comfortable sharing that, but I've researched the market range for this role and I'm confident we can find alignment."

### "What are your salary expectations?"
- "Based on my research and the scope of this role, I'm targeting the range of $X to $Y. Is that within your budget?"
- "I'm flexible and more focused on the right opportunity. What range have you budgeted for this role?"

## When You Must Answer
If pressed after deflecting twice:
- Give a range based on market research (not your current salary)
- Make the bottom of your range your actual target
- "Based on my research for this role in [location], I'm targeting $X to $Y, depending on the full compensation package."

## Key Points
- Your past salary is irrelevant to your future market value
- Research the market rate for the role, not what you currently make
- Never volunteer salary information unprompted
- If they insist, you can choose whether this is a company you want to work for
