# Informational Interview Guide

## The Core Idea

An informational interview is a 15-20 minute conversation with someone at a target company or in a target role. It is NOT a job interview — it is research. But done well, it often leads to referrals, introductions, and insider knowledge that gives you a significant edge.

## How to Request One

```
Subject: Quick question about [Role/Team] at [Company]

Hi [Name],

I'm a [your role/background] exploring [target area], and your work
at [Company] on [specific thing] caught my attention.

Would you have 15-20 minutes for a virtual coffee? I'd love to hear
about your experience on the [Team] and any advice you might have
for someone looking to move into this space.

Completely understand if your schedule doesn't allow it. Thanks either way.

[Your name]
```

## Questions to Ask

- What does a typical day/week look like in your role?
- What skills are most important for success in this role?
- What do you wish you had known before joining?
- What is the team culture like?
- What is the interview process like at [Company]?
- Is there anyone else you would recommend I speak with?

## Questions NOT to Ask
- "Can you get me a job?" (defeats the purpose)
- Anything about salary (too forward for this context)
- "Will you refer me?" (let it happen naturally — if the conversation goes well, they often offer)

## After the Conversation
- Send a thank-you email within 24 hours
- Connect on LinkedIn if you have not already
- Follow up if they offered introductions — do not let warm leads go cold
- If a relevant role opens up later, you now have a warm contact
