# LinkedIn Profile Optimization for Job Seekers

## The Core Idea

Your LinkedIn profile is your digital first impression. Recruiters search LinkedIn by keywords, so your profile needs to be optimized for the roles you are targeting — not just a copy of your resume.

## Key Optimizations

### Headline
- Do NOT use your current job title alone
- Use: "[Current Role] | [Key Skill] | [What You Do / Want to Do]"
- Example: "Data Analyst | SQL & Python | Turning Data into Business Decisions"
- Include keywords recruiters search for

### About Section
- Write in first person — this is not a resume
- Lead with what you do and what you are looking for
- Include keywords naturally
- End with a call to action ("Feel free to connect if...")

### Experience
- Mirror your resume but add more context and keywords
- LinkedIn bullets can be slightly longer than resume bullets
- Include media (presentations, projects, articles) where relevant

### Skills Section
- Add 50 skills (the maximum) — prioritize the ones matching your target role
- Pin your top 3 skills — these appear first and get endorsed most
- Skills directly affect LinkedIn search ranking

### Open to Work
- Turn on "Open to Work" — visible to recruiters only (not your current employer)
- Specify target role titles, locations, and work types

## Activity That Helps
- Comment thoughtfully on posts in your industry
- Share articles with your take
- Engage with content from people at your target companies
- Post about projects, learnings, or industry trends
