# How to Ask for a Referral

## The Core Idea

A referral gets your resume seen by a human immediately. Referred candidates are 3-5x more likely to be hired. The key is making the ask easy for the person referring you — give them everything they need.

## The Outreach Template

### For Someone You Know Well
```
Subject: Quick favor — [Company] [Role]

Hey [Name],

I saw that [Company] has a [Role Title] open and I'm really interested.
I know you're on the [Team/at Company] — would you be open to putting
in a referral for me?

I've attached my resume and here's the job link: [URL]

Happy to send you a quick blurb about why I'm a fit if that helps
with the referral form. Let me know — really appreciate it either way.

[Your name]
```

### For a Loose Connection
```
Subject: [Company] [Role] — would love your insight

Hi [Name],

We connected [context — event, mutual friend, LinkedIn]. I noticed
you're at [Company] and I'm interested in the [Role Title] position.

Would you have 15 minutes for a quick chat about what it's like
working there? I'd also love to know if you'd be comfortable
putting in a referral once you've had a chance to learn more about
my background.

Either way, I appreciate your time.

[Your name]
```

## Rules
1. **Make it easy.** Attach your resume and include the job link
2. **Be specific.** Name the exact role
3. **Do not pressure.** "Either way, I appreciate it" gives them an out
4. **Follow up once.** If no response after a week, send one gentle follow-up
5. **Always thank them.** Whether or not you get the job
6. **Close the loop.** Update them on the outcome
