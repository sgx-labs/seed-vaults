# Tell Me About Yourself — Framework

## The Core Idea

This is the most common interview opener and your chance to set the agenda. It is NOT an invitation to recite your resume chronologically. It is a 60-90 second pitch that connects your past to this specific role.

## The Three-Part Framework

### 1. Present (15-20 seconds)
What you are doing now and what you are good at.
"I'm currently a [role] at [company], where I focus on [key responsibility]. Most recently, I [notable recent accomplishment]."

### 2. Past (20-30 seconds)
The relevant backstory that brought you here.
"Before that, I spent [X years] at [company/industry] where I developed expertise in [skills relevant to this role]. A highlight was [accomplishment that maps to this job]."

### 3. Future (15-20 seconds)
Why you are here and why this role.
"What excites me about [this role/company] is [specific thing]. My experience in [skill] positions me well to [how you'd contribute], and I'm eager to [what you want to accomplish here]."

## Rules

1. **Under 90 seconds.** Practice with a timer.
2. **Tailor it to the role.** Emphasize different parts of your background for different positions.
3. **Do not recite your resume.** They have your resume. Tell the story behind it.
4. **End with why you are here.** This creates a natural transition to discussing the role.
5. **Be conversational.** This is not a speech. It is the start of a dialogue.

## Common Mistakes
- Starting with "Well, I was born in..." (too far back)
- Listing every job you have had (resume recitation)
- Being too vague ("I'm a hard worker who loves challenges")
- Forgetting to connect your story to this specific role
- Going over 2 minutes (you have lost them)
