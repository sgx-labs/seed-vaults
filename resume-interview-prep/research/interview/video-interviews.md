# Video Interview Best Practices

## The Core Idea

Video interviews are the default for at least the first round. The fundamentals of preparation and communication still apply, but the medium introduces new variables — lighting, audio, background, eye contact with a camera, and managing technical glitches.

## Setup

### Technical
- **Test your setup 30 minutes before** — camera, microphone, internet, platform
- **Use a wired connection** if possible (more reliable than WiFi)
- **Close unnecessary applications** — notifications, Slack, email
- **Have a backup plan** — phone number for the interviewer if tech fails
- **Use headphones** — reduces echo and background noise

### Visual
- **Camera at eye level** — stack books under your laptop if needed
- **Light your face** — window in front of you or a desk lamp. Never backlit.
- **Clean, neutral background** — bookshelf or plain wall. Avoid virtual backgrounds.
- **Dress fully** — yes, including pants. You may need to stand up.

### During the Interview
- **Look at the camera, not the screen** — this is "eye contact" on video
- **Nod and react visibly** — energy reads lower on video. Overcompensate slightly.
- **Pause before answering** — slight delay prevents talking over the interviewer
- **Keep notes nearby** — one advantage of video is having your prep sheet visible

## Common Mistakes
- Looking at yourself instead of the camera
- Fidgeting or swiveling (amplified on video)
- Forgetting to mute when not speaking in panel interviews
- Not having water nearby
