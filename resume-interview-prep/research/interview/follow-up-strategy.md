# Post-Interview Follow-Up Strategy

## The Core Idea

A thoughtful follow-up email within 24 hours reinforces your candidacy and keeps you top of mind. It is one of the easiest competitive advantages in a job search because most candidates either skip it or send something generic.

## The Thank-You Email

### Timing
- Send within 24 hours of the interview
- If you interviewed with multiple people, send individual emails to each
- If you do not have their email, ask the recruiter or find it on LinkedIn

### Structure
1. **Thank them** for their time and the conversation
2. **Reference something specific** discussed — proves you were engaged
3. **Reinforce your fit** — briefly connect your experience to a need they mentioned
4. **Express enthusiasm** — genuine, not desperate
5. **Keep it short** — 4-6 sentences total

### Example
```
Subject: Thank you — [Role Title] conversation

Hi [Name],

Thank you for taking the time to speak with me today about the [Role]
position. I really enjoyed our discussion about [specific topic from
interview].

Our conversation reinforced my excitement about this role, particularly
[specific aspect]. My experience with [relevant skill/project] aligns
well with the [challenge/goal they mentioned], and I am eager to
contribute.

Looking forward to the next steps. Please do not hesitate to reach out
if you need any additional information.

Best,
[Your name]
```

## Following Up After No Response

- **After 1 week** — short, friendly check-in with the recruiter
- **After 2 weeks** — one more follow-up, then move on mentally
- **Never follow up more than twice** — persistence becomes pestering
- **Keep applying elsewhere** — never pause your search for one opportunity
