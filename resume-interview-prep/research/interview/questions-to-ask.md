# Questions to Ask the Interviewer

## The Core Idea

"Do you have any questions for us?" is not a formality — it is an evaluation. Thoughtful questions demonstrate genuine interest, strategic thinking, and that you are evaluating them as much as they are evaluating you. Always have 3-5 prepared. Ask 2-3 based on what feels natural.

## Strong Questions by Category

### About the Role
- What does success look like in this role in the first 90 days?
- What are the biggest challenges the person in this role will face?
- How does this role contribute to the team's/company's larger goals?
- What would you want someone to accomplish in the first year?

### About the Team
- Can you tell me about the team I would be working with?
- How does the team collaborate day to day?
- What is the team's biggest priority right now?

### About Growth
- What does the career path look like for someone in this role?
- How does the company support professional development?
- Where have previous people in this role moved on to?

### About the Interviewer
- What do you enjoy most about working here?
- What has surprised you about working at [company]?
- How has your role evolved since you joined?

### About the Company
- What is the company's biggest strategic priority this year?
- How has [recent company news/change] affected the team?

## Questions to Avoid
- Anything you could easily Google (founding date, CEO name, headquarters)
- Salary, benefits, or PTO in early rounds (save for offer stage)
- "When will I hear back?" (appropriate for the recruiter, not the hiring manager)
- Yes/no questions that kill the conversation
