# Action Verbs and Power Words for Resumes

## The Core Idea

The first word of every bullet point sets the tone. Strong action verbs convey confidence and ownership. Weak verbs ("helped," "assisted," "participated") signal a supporting role. Choose verbs that match the level of impact you had.

## Verbs by Category

### Leadership & Management
Directed, Led, Managed, Oversaw, Supervised, Mentored, Coached, Recruited, Delegated, Mobilized, Championed, Spearheaded

### Achievement & Delivery
Achieved, Delivered, Exceeded, Surpassed, Completed, Earned, Won, Attained, Outperformed, Produced

### Analysis & Problem-Solving
Analyzed, Assessed, Diagnosed, Evaluated, Identified, Investigated, Resolved, Forecasted, Modeled, Audited

### Communication & Collaboration
Presented, Negotiated, Influenced, Persuaded, Partnered, Collaborated, Facilitated, Advised, Consulted, Mediated

### Process & Efficiency
Streamlined, Automated, Consolidated, Optimized, Redesigned, Simplified, Standardized, Integrated, Transformed, Overhauled

### Growth & Development
Expanded, Grew, Increased, Launched, Scaled, Built, Established, Pioneered, Initiated, Developed

### Technical & Creation
Engineered, Architected, Designed, Implemented, Configured, Deployed, Programmed, Migrated, Built, Developed

## Verbs to Avoid
- Helped, Assisted (passive — what specifically did you do?)
- Responsible for, Tasked with (duties, not achievements)
- Participated in (were you a bystander?)
- Worked on (too vague — what exactly?)
- Utilized (just say "used")
