# How to Tailor Your Resume to a Specific Job Posting

## The Core Idea

A tailored resume mirrors the language, priorities, and requirements of the job posting. Hiring managers and ATS systems are looking for alignment between your experience and their needs. Tailoring is not lying — it is selecting and emphasizing the parts of your real experience that are most relevant.

## The Process

### Step 1: Decode the Job Posting

Read the posting three times:
1. **First read** — overall feel. What kind of person are they looking for?
2. **Second read** — highlight hard requirements (must-haves vs nice-to-haves)
3. **Third read** — extract specific keywords, tools, and skills mentioned

### Step 2: Create a Keyword Map

List every skill, tool, technology, and qualification mentioned in the posting. Note how many times each appears — frequency signals priority.

```
Example keyword map:
- "data analysis" (mentioned 4x) → HIGH PRIORITY
- "stakeholder management" (3x) → HIGH PRIORITY
- "SQL" (2x) → MEDIUM
- "Python" (1x) → INCLUDE
- "cross-functional" (2x) → MEDIUM
```

### Step 3: Mirror the Language

If the posting says "stakeholder management," do not write "client relations." Use their exact terminology. ATS systems match keywords literally.

### Step 4: Reorder Your Bullets

Put the most relevant accomplishments first under each role. The top 2 bullets under each job should directly address the posting's top priorities.

### Step 5: Customize Your Summary

Your professional summary (if you use one) should read like a direct response to the posting's first paragraph. Echo their priorities back to them.

## Common Mistakes

- **Keyword stuffing** — Listing keywords without context. ATS may pass you, but humans will reject you.
- **One resume for all applications** — The fastest way to get filtered out.
- **Ignoring soft skills** — If the posting emphasizes "collaboration" or "communication," your bullets should demonstrate these.
- **Not reading the full posting** — Requirements at the bottom matter. Preferred qualifications tell you what they really want.

## Time Investment

Tailoring should take 20-30 minutes per application. If you are spending less, you are not tailoring enough. If more, you are overthinking it.
