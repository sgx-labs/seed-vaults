# Cover Letter Opening Lines That Get Read

## The Core Idea

The opening line determines whether the hiring manager reads the rest. Generic openings ("I am writing to apply for...") get skimmed over. A strong opener creates curiosity or demonstrates immediate relevance.

## Openers That Work

### The Referral Drop
"[Name] on your team suggested I reach out about the [Role] position — after hearing about [specific project/challenge], I knew my experience in [skill] could make an immediate impact."

### The Specific Connection
"When I read about [Company]'s [specific initiative or product], I saw a direct parallel to the work I did at [your company], where I [relevant accomplishment]."

### The Problem-Solver
"You need someone who can [key requirement from posting]. In my current role, I have been doing exactly that — [brief proof]."

### The Passionate Expert
"I have spent the last [X] years focused on [domain], and [Company]'s approach to [specific thing] is the most compelling I have seen in the industry."

## Openers to Avoid

- "I am writing to apply for the position of..." (boring, obvious)
- "I believe I would be a great fit..." (everyone says this)
- "To whom it may concern..." (signals you did zero research)
- Starting with your life story
- Starting with a quote or joke (too risky)
