# Cover Letter Structure That Works

## The Core Idea

A great cover letter does three things: hooks the reader, demonstrates fit, and makes a clear ask. It should take 2-3 minutes to read and leave the hiring manager wanting to look at your resume more closely.

## The Three-Paragraph Structure

### Paragraph 1: The Hook (2-3 sentences)
- Name the role you are applying for
- State why THIS company (not just any company)
- Reference something specific — a product, value, recent news, or mission
- Optional: mention a mutual connection or referral

**Example:**
"I'm writing to express my interest in the Senior Data Analyst role at [Company]. Your team's work on [specific initiative] aligns directly with my passion for using data to drive customer-centric decisions, and I'd welcome the opportunity to contribute."

### Paragraph 2: The Bridge (3-5 sentences)
- Your strongest relevant accomplishment (with a number)
- How your background maps to their needs
- A specific skill or experience that addresses a requirement in the posting
- This should not repeat your resume — it should add context and narrative

### Paragraph 3: The Close (2-3 sentences)
- What you would bring to the team
- Express enthusiasm
- Clear call to action ("I welcome the opportunity to discuss...")
- Thank them for their time

## Rules
- **Under one page.** Always.
- **Customize the first paragraph for every application.** If you cannot, skip the cover letter.
- **Match the tone of the company.** Formal for banks and enterprise, slightly more casual for startups.
- **Proofread ruthlessly.** A typo in a cover letter is a disqualifier.
