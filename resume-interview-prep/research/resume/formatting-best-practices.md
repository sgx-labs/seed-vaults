# Resume Formatting Best Practices

## The Core Idea

Resume formatting should be invisible. The reader should absorb your content without ever noticing the design. Clean, consistent formatting signals professionalism. Cluttered or creative formatting signals that you are compensating for weak content.

## Layout Guidelines

### One Page vs Two Pages
- **Under 10 years experience** — one page
- **10+ years or senior roles** — two pages are acceptable
- **Never three pages** — unless you are an academic or medical professional
- A strong one-page resume beats a padded two-page resume every time

### Margins and Spacing
- Margins: 0.5" to 1" on all sides
- Font size: 10-12pt for body, 12-14pt for name
- Line spacing: 1.0 to 1.15
- Consistent spacing between sections

### Section Order (Chronological — Most Common)
1. Name and contact information
2. Professional summary (optional — skip if it would be generic)
3. Experience (reverse chronological)
4. Education
5. Skills / Certifications
6. Additional (volunteer work, publications — only if relevant)

### Contact Information
- Full name
- City, State (no street address)
- Phone number
- Professional email
- LinkedIn URL (customized)
- Portfolio/GitHub if relevant

## Common Formatting Mistakes
- Inconsistent date formatting (May 2020 vs 5/2020 vs 2020)
- Inconsistent bullet styles or indentation
- Orphaned headers (section title at bottom of page, content on next)
- Too many font styles or sizes
- Walls of text without white space
