# Handling Employment Gaps on Your Resume

## The Core Idea

Employment gaps are far more common and accepted than they used to be, especially post-2020. The key is not hiding gaps but framing them honestly and positively. What matters is what you did during the gap and that you are ready to contribute now.

## Strategies

### Short Gaps (Under 6 Months)
- Use years only instead of months on your resume (2021-2023 instead of Jan 2021 - Mar 2023)
- Often no explanation needed — job transitions take time

### Longer Gaps (6+ Months)
- **Freelance/consulting** — If you did any contract work, list it
- **Education/certification** — Any courses, certifications, or training
- **Caregiving** — "Family caregiver" is a legitimate line item. No further detail needed.
- **Health** — "Career break for personal reasons" is sufficient. Never share medical details.
- **Volunteering** — Relevant volunteer work shows continued engagement

### In the Interview
- Acknowledge it briefly and confidently
- Pivot immediately to what you learned or how you stayed current
- Never apologize or over-explain
- "I took time to [reason], and during that period I [productive activity]. I'm energized and ready to [what you'll bring to this role]."

## What Not to Do
- Do not lie or fabricate employment
- Do not leave it completely unaddressed if asked
- Do not be defensive — confidence signals that you have moved past it
