# Writing Resume Bullet Points That Get Interviews

## The Core Idea

Every bullet point should answer the question: "So what?" A duty describes what you were supposed to do. An accomplishment describes what happened because you did it. Hiring managers want accomplishments.

## The Formula

```
[Action Verb] + [What You Did] + [Scope/Scale] + [Result/Impact]
```

### Bad vs Good

| Bad (Duty) | Good (Accomplishment) |
|------------|----------------------|
| Responsible for managing team | Led 12-person cross-functional team to deliver $3.2M product launch 2 weeks ahead of schedule |
| Handled customer complaints | Resolved 95% of escalated customer issues within 24 hours, improving CSAT scores from 3.2 to 4.6 |
| Created reports | Built automated reporting dashboard used by 40+ stakeholders, eliminating 15 hours/week of manual reporting |
| Worked on process improvement | Redesigned onboarding workflow, reducing new-hire ramp time from 8 weeks to 4 weeks |

## How to Quantify When You Think You Cannot

Everything is quantifiable. Ask yourself:
- **How many?** People, projects, clients, tickets, systems
- **How much?** Revenue, cost savings, budget managed
- **How fast?** Time saved, deadlines met, turnaround improved
- **How often?** Frequency, consistency, reliability
- **Compared to what?** Before/after, vs. benchmark, vs. team average

If you truly cannot find a number, use scope indicators: "enterprise-level," "company-wide," "across 3 departments."

## Power Verbs by Category

**Leadership:** Spearheaded, Directed, Orchestrated, Championed, Mobilized
**Achievement:** Delivered, Exceeded, Surpassed, Achieved, Earned
**Efficiency:** Streamlined, Automated, Consolidated, Optimized, Accelerated
**Growth:** Expanded, Grew, Increased, Scaled, Launched
**Problem-solving:** Resolved, Diagnosed, Transformed, Overhauled, Pioneered

## Rules

1. **Start every bullet with a strong action verb.** Never start with "Responsible for" or "Helped with."
2. **One accomplishment per bullet.** Do not cram two achievements into one line.
3. **Most impactful bullets first.** Recruiters read top-down and often stop after 2-3 bullets per role.
4. **3-5 bullets per role.** More than 5 dilutes your strongest points.
5. **Recent roles get more bullets.** Current/last role: 4-5. Two jobs ago: 3-4. Older: 2-3.
