# ATS Optimization — Getting Past the Robots

## The Core Idea

75%+ of resumes are rejected by ATS before a human sees them. ATS systems parse your resume into structured data fields and score keyword matches against the job description. Your resume must be machine-readable first, human-impressive second.

## Formatting Rules

### Do
- Use standard section headers: "Experience," "Education," "Skills," "Summary"
- Use .docx or .pdf (check which the application accepts)
- Use standard fonts (Arial, Calibri, Times New Roman, Garamond)
- Use simple bullet points (•, -, or standard list formatting)
- Include your name and contact info at the top (not in a header/footer)

### Do Not
- Use tables, columns, or text boxes (ATS cannot parse them reliably)
- Use images, icons, or graphics
- Put critical info in headers or footers (many ATS skip these)
- Use creative section names ("Where I've Made Impact" instead of "Experience")
- Use abbreviations without also spelling them out ("PM / Project Management")

## Keyword Strategy

1. **Match exact phrases** from the job posting — "project management" not "managing projects"
2. **Include both acronyms and full terms** — "SQL (Structured Query Language)"
3. **Place keywords in context** — in bullet points, not just a skills list
4. **Include keywords in multiple sections** — skills section AND experience bullets
5. **Do not keyword stuff** — ATS penalizes unnatural repetition

## Testing Your Resume

- Copy your resume text into a plain text editor. If it reads coherently, ATS can parse it.
- Use the job posting as a checklist. Highlight every requirement you have addressed.
- If you hit 60%+ keyword match, you are in good shape. Below 40%, revise.
